# America move preparation

**Parallel readiness track** for a possible US move: communication polish, culture/history/civics orientation, and practical move research — without premature filings or committed identity documents in the vault.

## Goal / Outcome

If/when a US opportunity is real: you can **communicate at professional standard**, navigate **culture and civic basics**, and hold a **clear personal brief** (where / why / constraints / open questions) — not a panic scramble.

## Why / Life Area Alignment

Career leverage (labs, frontier companies, network density) may pull toward the US. Preparation is a **Career + Learning** project so Sweden integration and US optionality both stay visible without thrashing either.

---

## Track A — Communication readiness

English is already strong for tech; raise *US professional + social* fluency.

- **Speech:** Record one 3-minute mock intro (“who I am, what I ship, what I want”) in US-meeting style; re-record weekly until tight.
- **Write:** Practice short US-style emails (subject line + 5 sentences + clear ask) for outreach and applications.
- **Read:** Skim one US tech or local-news piece for target cities weekly (tone + issues, not doomscroll).
- **Idioms / norms:** Note phrases that differ from EU English; avoid over-formal academic register in chat.

**Done when:** Intro monologue feels natural; one outreach email template you’re proud of lives in a private note (not committed secrets).

## Track B — Culture, history, civics (orientation)

Enough context to participate and avoid blind spots — not a citizenship exam cram (unless you later need that path).

| Pillar | Practice |
|--------|----------|
| **Culture** | Workplace norms (directness, self-promotion balance, networking), tip/service culture, regional variation (coast vs midwest vs south — pick targets first) |
| **History** | Short arc: founding documents → federal system → 20th-century superpower + civil rights → present polarization *as map, not identity war* |
| **Civics** | Federal vs state vs local; elections basics; what “at-will employment” and healthcare fragmentation mean for a worker |
| **Daily life** | Transit vs car defaults, credit history, SSN *concept only*, housing lease norms — research when city is chosen |

**Done when:** You can explain federal vs state power and *why your target city* fits you, in under 3 minutes.

## Track C — Practical move readiness (checklist only)

No applications filed from this card; research and decisions only.

- [ ] **Target brief:** 1–3 cities/regions with why (roles, network, COL, climate, visa feasibility)
- [ ] **Work path inventory:** list plausible categories to research (e.g. employer sponsorship, intracompany, exceptional ability, other) — *names of paths only*; verify with official sources later
- [ ] **Cost envelope:** rough monthly burn + 3–6 month runway target for chosen city (use [[Projects/wealth-dd/README|wealth-dd]] privately)
- [ ] **Career surface:** CV + LinkedIn/devprofile aligned to US role language ([[Projects/latex-cv/README|latex-cv]], [[Projects/devprofile/README|devprofile]], [[Projects/collab-finder/README|collab-finder]])
- [ ] **Timeline sketch:** earliest realistic move window *if* an offer exists (not a deadline fantasy)
- [ ] **Open questions list:** legal, tax dual-residency, SE exit logistics — for human expert later

**Privacy:** Never put passport numbers, full SSN, case IDs, or unredacted immigration packets in life-os git. Private folder or offline only.
