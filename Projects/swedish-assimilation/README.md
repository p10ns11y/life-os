# Swedish cultural assimilation

**Practical integration playbook** for living well in Sweden: Swedish **essentials** (read · speech · write), plus **culture** and **history** that unlock social and civic fit. Not identity erasure — competent participation.

| | |
|--|--|
| Area | [[Learning]] (primary) · [[Career]] (work Swedish + society norms) |
| Horizon | Ongoing; weekly practice beats intensive binges |
| Related | [[Projects/america-move-prep/README\|america-move-prep]] (parallel US track) |

## Goal / Outcome

Comfortable daily life in Swedish: understand news and forms, hold real conversations, write clear messages, and navigate norms with historical context — without freezing into English-only bubbles.

## Why / Life Area Alignment

You already live in the Swedish context (finance docs, society surface). Language + cultural fluency compound career, relationships, and agency. This card makes assimilation **scheduled and trackable**, not a vague guilt item.

---

## Essentials — Read

Build passive → active literacy for real surfaces (news, mail, contracts, chat).

- **Daily micro:** 10–20 min of graded or real Swedish (SVT Nyheter Lätt, 8 Sidor, or one section of a normal article).
- **Active mark-up:** Underline unknown words; add only 5–10 to a personal list (no infinite flashcard hoard).
- **Forms & mail:** When official Swedish mail arrives, force a first-pass read before English translation.
- **Books later:** Short fiction / YA only after news + diary feel steady.

**Done when (this month):** Can summarize a short news piece in Swedish (orally or written) without looking up every sentence.

## Essentials — Speech

Speaking is the bottleneck; schedule mouth-time, not more apps.

- **Shadowing:** Play 1–2 min of Swedish audio (radio/podcast); repeat aloud same rhythm.
- **Daily out-loud:** Read your diary or 5 news sentences **aloud** every session.
- **Real friction:** One Swedish-only micro-interaction per day when possible (cashier, neighbor, colleague hello + one sentence).
- **Practice partner / SFI:** If enrolled or eligible, treat class as non-negotiable reps; if not, language café or tandem once/week.

**Done when (this month):** 2-minute monologue about your week without switching to English mid-sentence more than twice.

## Essentials — Write

Writing freezes vocabulary into usable form.

- **5-line diary** in Swedish after each practice block (date + what you did + one feeling + one plan).
- **Messages:** Prefer Swedish for short texts to Swedish contacts when stakes are low.
- **Corrections:** Paste one paragraph into a checker *after* you write; rewrite once by hand — do not start in the tool.
- **Work surface:** Keep a private glossary of job/domain Swedish terms you actually need.

**Done when (this month):** One full diary week without English fallback lines.

## Culture → integration

How Swedes coordinate life — learn by observation + small experiments.

| Theme | Practice |
|-------|----------|
| **Lagom / Jantelagen awareness** | Notice understatement in meetings; practice short, factual turns instead of hard sell |
| **Punctuality & queues** | Arrive early; treat time boxes as social contracts |
| **Coffee / fika** | Use fika as relationship time, not only caffeine |
| **Nature & outdoor default** | One outdoor walk/week as cultural habit, not “exercise only” |
| **Consensus & conflict style** | Prefer calm disagreement + proposal over confrontation theater |
| **Privacy norms** | Don’t overshare personal finance/status early; match peer depth |

**Integration check:** Can name 3 workplace or social norms you now follow deliberately (log in a session note).

## History → integration

Enough history to understand *why* institutions and attitudes feel the way they do — not a degree track.

| Track | Focus | Practice |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Modern welfare state** | Folkhemmet, post-war social model, high trust | Read one short explainer; note 3 institutions you use (Försäkringskassan, Skatteverket, region healthcare) |
| **20th century turns** | Neutrality narrative, EU membership, immigration waves | One documentary or chapter; write 5 sentences on what changed |
| **Local place** | Your kommun / city’s short history | Visit one museum or local heritage page; one paragraph in session log |
| **Language history** | Why English is strong yet Swedish remains civic glue | Observe code-switching; choose Swedish for low-stakes civic moments |

**Done when:** You can explain to a newcomer (in English or Swedish) *why* high trust + strong institutions matter here, in under 2 minutes.
